Gym beginners guide

As a beginner, start with light weights and focus on learning proper form. Don't try to lift too much too soon. It's crucial to begin with basic exercises like squats, deadlifts, and bench presses, which target multiple muscle groups. Aim for consistency and gradually increase intensity.

A beginner's gym routine should include a mix of cardio and strength training. You can find free workout plans online, or consider working with a trainer to develop a routine that fits your goals. Start with 3-4 days of exercise per week, focusing on different muscle groups each day.

A beginner should aim to spend about 45 minutes to an hour at the gym. This includes warm-up, the workout itself, and a cool-down period. It's important not to overdo it initially—focus on building a habit rather than exhausting yourself.

On your first visit to the gym, start with a warm-up on a treadmill or stationary bike for 5-10 minutes. Then, do some bodyweight exercises like squats, lunges, and push-ups. Finish with a cooldown and light stretching. Focus on getting comfortable with the environment and equipment.

The best gym routine depends on your goals. A good all-around routine involves a combination of strength training and cardio. Focus on compound exercises like squats, deadlifts, and presses, and alternate between upper and lower body workouts. Make sure to include rest days for recovery.
